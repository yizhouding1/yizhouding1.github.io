Manuscript under reviewComputational finance · Numerical PDEs

A Variable-Step Crank-Nicolson and High-Order Compact Scheme for a Nonlinear Black-Scholes Type Equation

Yizhou Ding, Xingyu Zhou, and Pin Lyu

We develop a fully discrete linear scheme for a nonlinear Black-Scholes type equation with counterparty risk. The method combines variable-step Crank-Nicolson time discretization, a fourth-order compact spatial operator, and an explicit treatment of nonlinear terms, with stability, convergence, and numerical performance examined under alternative grids.

Galaxy Futures · 2024–2025Options · High-frequency data

Short-horizon volatility returns versus option time decay

Investigated whether intraday volatility capture can offset option theta decay using high-frequency crude-oil and soybean-meal options data. The project separated empirical pattern identification from strategy evaluation, then stated data, timing, and backtesting assumptions explicitly before interpreting the results.
